# CRYPTO STONE WP v2.9 EN

Bitcoin formed a new asset concept known as “digital gold” through limited supply, publicly disclosed mining rules, a decentralized network, and a transaction structure that anyone can verify, even though it is not directly backed by physical gold. The essential significance of Bitcoin lies in proving that scarcity and ownership of a digital asset can be implemented through code and a network, without relying on the trust of a specific issuer or central institution.

The author seeks to extend this philosophy into the asset concept of gemstones. In the real world, gemstones have limited mining quantities, and each gemstone has different scarcity and value depending on attributes such as weight, color, clarity, and cut. Even gemstones of the same type can have different values if their individual attributes differ. In this sense, gemstones are inherently non-fungible and have characteristics suitable for representation as unique assets in a digital environment.

CryptoStone is a decentralized digital gemstone protocol designed to express the mineability, scarcity, grading, and collectibility of gemstones as on-chain data, and to allow gemstone NFTs to be mined in a digital environment without arbitrary intervention by a central server or operator. Each gemstone is represented as a unique Gem NFT, and attributes such as stone type, weight, color, clarity, cut, mining time, and the mining pool from which it was mined are recorded on-chain.

This project does not aim to provide ownership, collateral rights, or redemption rights to real gemstones. What the author proposes in this whitepaper is a study and experiment to implement the structural characteristics of real gemstones — namely limitation, mineability, grading, scarcity, and collectibility — as independent scarce assets in a digital environment.

If Bitcoin created the narrative of digital gold, and Litecoin was called digital silver, CryptoStone presents a new asset category: digital gemstones that are mined and refined on smart contracts.
